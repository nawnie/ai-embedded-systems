Embedded AI Systems

We design embedded AI systems that keep perception and reasoning close to the hardware. The goal is practical onboard intelligence: less dependency on remote services, lower latency, and better control over data and behavior.

  • Onboard inference measured against latency and power limits
  • Local decision support with defined human authority
  • Memory and telemetry that make behavior inspectable
  • Hardware-aware integration and failure handling

Where embedded AI earns its place

Keeping inference near the equipment can reduce latency, limit data movement, and preserve useful behavior when connectivity is poor. It also creates hard constraints: compute capacity, heat, power, model size, update paths, and the need to explain what happens when confidence is low.

We begin with the operational decision, the available signals, and the acceptable failure modes. A first engagement can produce a hardware and runtime recommendation, a bounded prototype, or a feasibility decision backed by measurements rather than a broad AI promise.
